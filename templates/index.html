What is Dyslexia?

Dyslexia is a learning disorder characterized by difficulty reading due to problems identifying speech sounds and learning how they relate to letters and words. It affects areas of the brain that process language.

People with dyslexia have normal intelligence and usually have normal vision. Most children with dyslexia can succeed in school with tutoring or a specialized education program. Emotional support also plays an important role.

Common Signs of Dyslexia:

  • Late talking
  • Learning new words slowly
  • Problems forming words correctly
  • Difficulty reading and writing
  • Trouble understanding rapid instructions
  • Problems remembering the sequence of things
  • Difficulty seeing similarities and differences in letters and words
Early Detection Matters

Early identification of dyslexia is crucial for effective intervention. Our multi-modal detection system analyzes eye movements, handwriting patterns, and speech characteristics to identify potential indicators of dyslexia.
